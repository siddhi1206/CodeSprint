Why Organ Donation Matters

Understanding the importance of becoming an organ donor

Save Lives

A single organ donor can save up to 8 lives through donation of heart, lungs, liver, kidneys, pancreas and intestines.

Restore Sight

Cornea donations can restore sight to those suffering from corneal blindness or injury.

Heal Lives

Tissue donations like skin, bone, and heart valves can help burn victims, cancer patients, and those needing reconstructive surgery.

The Organ Donation Process

Organ donation is a process where a person allows an organ of their own to be removed legally and transplanted into another person through surgery. Organs that can be donated include:

  • Heart: For patients with end-stage heart failure
  • Lungs: For patients with various forms of end-stage lung disease
  • Liver: For patients with end-stage liver failure
  • Kidneys: For patients with end-stage renal disease
  • Pancreas: For patients with type 1 diabetes
  • Eyes: Corneas can restore sight to the blind

The donation process involves careful matching of donors and recipients based on blood type, tissue type, organ size, medical urgency, and geographic location.
